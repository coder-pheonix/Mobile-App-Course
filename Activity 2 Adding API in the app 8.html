Splash Screen 2 of "Weather Forecasting App"

Now, we will code for Screen 2 of the Weather Forecasting App.

The steps to code for the Screen 2 are as follows.

  1. Go to the "Functions" and drag the "to do something" on the screen.
  2. Change the "do something" to "getWeather".
  3. Go to the "Functions" and drag the "getWeather" on the screen.
  4. Go to the "WebAPIs" and click on the "Web_API1".
  5. Change the "Web_API1" to "OpenWeatherMapAPI".
  6. Click on the submit.
  7. Go to the "OpenWeatherMapAPI" and drag the "set OpenWeatherMapAPI's URL".
  8. Go to the "Text" and drag the "join" on the screen.
  9. Click on the icon on "join" block.
  10. Add "item" to the join list.
  11. Again click on the icon to minimize the join list.
  12. Go to the "SelectCityButton" and drag the "SelectCityButton's text" on the screen.
  13. Delete the "world" block.
  14. Go to the "Text" and drag the first block on the screen.
  15. Write in the block "&units=".
  16. Go to the "Text" and drag the first block on the screen.
  17. Write in the block "imperial".
  18. Go to the "Text" and drag the first block on the screen.
  19. Write in the block "&APPID=".
  20. Go to the "Variables" and drag the "app variable APIKey" block.
  21. Go to the "OpenWeatherMapAPI" and drag the "call OpenWeatherMapAPI's Get" on the screen.
  22. Go to the "Variables" and drag the "set app variable APIKey to" block.
  23. Select "app variable temprature" from the list.
  24. Go to the "Objects" and drag the "get property" block.
  25. Change the "property name" to "temp".
  26. Go to the "Objects" and drag the "get property" block.
  27. Change the "property name" to "main".
  28. Go to the "Objects" and drag the "get object from JSON" block.
  29. Drag the "response" and place it at "get object from JSON" block.
  30. Go to the "TempratureLabel" and drag the "set TempratureLabel's Text to" block.
  31. Go to the "Text" and drag the "join" on the screen.
  32. Go to the "Variables" and drag the "app variable temprature" block.
